What is Scenario Comparison?

Scenario Comparison lets you freeze two different versions of your media plan and compare them side-by-side. Use it to evaluate the impact of budget changes, a different channel mix, or an alternative campaign strategy — before committing to anything.

Example uses: Compare a Rs 10L budget vs Rs 20L budget · Compare aggressive LinkedIn spend vs broader Meta mix · Compare 1-month vs 3-month campaign projections.

1
Set up your first planConfigure budget, channels, and parameters above. Give this scenario a name, then click "Save as Scenario A".
2
Change something meaningfulTry increasing the total budget, shifting budget between channels, or switching the campaign type. Then save as Scenario B.
3
See the full comparisonA detailed side-by-side breakdown appears instantly — showing what changed, which scenario delivers more results, and by how much.

How to Read These Numbers
Methodology, Accuracy & Assumptions
▼ Show
Are these output numbers accurate, or just predictions?
All output metrics — Impressions, Reach, Clicks, CPC, Leads/Purchases, and CPR — are directional projections, not guaranteed outcomes. They are calculated using India market benchmark averages for 2025–26, sourced from industry data across platforms like Meta, Google, and LinkedIn.

Think of them as a planning range, not a contractual promise. Real campaigns will vary based on actual conditions.
Impressions & Reach
±10–15%
High confidence. CPM is a platform rate card — actual delivery is close to model once campaign is live.
Clicks & CPC
±20–35%
Moderate confidence. CTR varies with creative quality, audience targeting, and ad fatigue — benchmark is a starting point.
Leads / Purchases & CPR
±30–50%
Lower confidence. Conversion rate is heavily influenced by landing page quality, offer strength, brand recall, and funnel design — factors outside the ad platform.
Key assumptions in every calculation
→ CPM, CTR, CVR values are based on India market averages across industries. Your actual rates will differ by brand maturity, audience quality, and creative.
→ The model assumes full budget utilisation — ad platforms do not always spend 100% of allocated budget, especially in niche audiences.
→ CPR is constant across budget scale when channel mix percentages and benchmarks are unchanged. Scaling budget does not automatically lower CPR.
→ Frequency and Reach calculations assume uniform audience distribution — real-world retargeting and interest-based audiences may overlap differently.
→ No seasonality adjustments are applied. CPMs and CTRs are typically higher during festival periods (Oct–Nov) in India.
→ D2C Revenue figures use AOV (Average Order Value) inputs. Actual revenue depends on return rates, repeat purchases, and cart abandonment.
How to present this to management
Present the outputs as planning estimates based on India platform benchmarks. Use them to compare channel mix options and budget scales — the relative differences between scenarios are far more reliable than the absolute numbers. When a scenario shows 5x more leads, that directional insight holds even if the exact number shifts by ±30% in reality.

Recommended framing for management: "Based on India market benchmarks, a Rs X budget across this channel mix is projected to generate approximately Y–Z leads at a CPR of Rs A–B. We will track against these benchmarks weekly and optimise allocations accordingly."

Default benchmarks are India market averages (2025–26). Adjust inputs based on your actual campaign data.
